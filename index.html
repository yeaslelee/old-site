About Me

Hello! I’m a first-stage researcher with a strong academic background and rigorous training in research methodologies. My general research interests are culture & diversity psychology, socioecology, intergroup relations, youth development, and conflict resolutions. Mainly leveraging R, I enjoy managing, analyzing, and visualizing datasets and interpreting results to answer research questions and to find evidence-based solutions.

My curiosity with how the world and people function began at an early age. It led me to explore the world in the forms of studying and traveling abroad since adolescence, building great resilience and independence. As of today, I continue my journey on developing a better understanding of the hows and whys of human behaviors. I believe that strong, theoretically grounded, and empirically sophisticated research is a crucial part of both understanding and solving problems in societies. My professional goal is to contribute to the betterment of society and the environment by conducting more impactful research.

On a more personal note, I love going on trips and exploring the new place by visiting local art museums and by hiking in nature. On my free time, I enjoy visiting museums and exhibitions, nurturing my spirituality through practicing meditation and yoga, and taking pictures of people and things that ‘spark joy’.

Research

Laboratory of Intergroup Relations and the Social Mind at Columbia University

Muslim Inter-Scholastic Tournament Study (Master’s Thesis)

A study exploring the spread of civic engagement among Muslim American adolescents over the course of a inter-scholastic tournament using a religion-based social network dataset. The spread was suggested as a potential mechanism underlying the relationships among among civic engagement, religiosity, and network centrality.

  • Responsibilities: quantitative analysis (regression modeling), data visualization, social network analysis, development of hypotheses, data management, data cleansing
  • Type of data: quantitative data (social network, socioeconomic demographics, behavioral measures)

STEM Attrition Study

A two-year-long project predicting retention and attrition of PhD students in STEM fields.

  • Responsibilities: participant recruitment, data collection, running information sessions, participant compensation
  • Type of data: quantitative data (longitudinal/daily diary measures of behaviors, thoughts, and emotions)

The Obama Effect Study

An intervention study investigating the potential beneficial effects of “symbolic firsts” on preventing racial/ethnic minority youth from stereotype threats and on promoting their academic performance.

  • Responsibilities: data management, codebook creation, IRB submission, participant recruitment, data collection, focus study design, literature review
  • Type of data: qualitative data (focus group) & quantitative data (socioeconomic demographics, academic performance, stereotype threat)

Merrilees Lab at SUNY Geneseo

Intergroup Contact & Well-being Study

A study examining the effects of quality and quantity of intergroup interactions on psychological well-being and stress among college students

  • Responsibilities: data entry, data management, data analysis
  • Type of data: quantitative data (daily diary/longitudinal measures on intergroup interactions, emotions, stress)

Youth & Families in Armed Conflict Study

A review article on the effects of armed conflict and political violence on youth and families

Merrilees, C. E., & Lee, Y. (2018). The role of emotional security, narrative, and resilience for youth and families in contexts of armed conflict. Family Court Review, 56(2), 248-257.
